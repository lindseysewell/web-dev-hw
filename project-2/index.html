Materials

  • Modeling clay, various colors
  • Detail items such as googly eyes, glitter, etc
  • Computer
  • Camera or webcam
  • Stop motion program such as iStopMotion
  • A friend to help you (optional)
  • Snacks (optional)

Instructions

  1. Create a character.
  2. Create a story for your character.
  3. Shape a figure of your character out of modeling clay.
  4. Snack Break!1
  5. Create your background.
  6. Put your character in the setting and pose them.
  7. Take a photo (with the help of your friend) and pose again.
  8. Repeat steps 6 & 7 with progressive positions to create your animation.
  9. Save, export, and share!

1. Make sure you feed your clay friends to keep up morale.

Extra Tips

  • Characters don't have to be too complex for a short animation. Even an amorphous blob can tell a fun story.
  • When creating your character, it helps to make several variations for facial expressions, positions, etc.
  • Experimenting with the clay as you work can help develop your story and make dynamic animations.
  • Even short scenes take many frames, but don't be discouraged! It doesn't take that long to get into a groove and make something cool. 96 frames can make about an 8 second animation in iStopMotion.

Conclusion
